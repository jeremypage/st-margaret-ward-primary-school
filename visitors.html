Visitors to our school
We are always pleased to welcome visitors to our school and here are details of just a few of our most recent guests:

RECEPTION WELCOME A NEW BABY
Our Reception children had special visitors recently. Baileigh's mum and her little baby brother Laiton, who is 16 weeks old, came in to spend an afternoon with us. The children were shown how to hold a baby correctly and how to look after Laiton properly. The children asked lots of interesting questions such as, 'What food does Laiton eat?' and 'Can Laiton talk?'. The whole class enjoyed the visit and even tried some of the baby food Baileigh's mum left for us!

KINETIC THEATRE GROUP
On Thursday 26 September our school was visited by the Kinetic Theatre Company. Children in Years 1-6 took part in their production, 'The Fillacavity Hotel' which taught the children about teeth and nutrition. A great time was had by all, with the children asking KTC when they would be back!

COOKERY DEMONSTRATION
On Friday 5 July, Professional Chef Pete delivered a cookery demonstration for the whole school. He talked about healthy eating and with the help of some keen volunteers, showed us how to make delicious, healthy recipes to try at home.

SKILL SHARE DAY
As part of our Economic Wellbeing Week(1-5 July 2013) we invited parents and friends to come into school for our Skills Share Day. The aim of the session was to enable pupils in Years 5 and 6 to find out more about the world of work and the kind of jobs people do, directly from real people. Our visitors talked to the children about how they got on at school;the qualifications they need for the work they do; any other jobs they have had as well as the best and worst aspects of their job.
We had a wonderful response from our parents,local businesses and the children and our speakers included a musician,British Gas Engineer, Accountant, School Nurse, Nursery Nurse, Psychiatrist, Mechanic, IT Manager, PSV Technician, Admin Assistant and PA to MCFC.

AUTHOR VISIT
Our St Margaret Ward children were delighted to welcome back author of' Darkmoor', Victoria Barry. She returned on 21 May 2013 with the great news that her novel is to be turned into a film and that we were going to assist in this by creating story boards for her to take to the film maker. Year 5 and Year 6 had a wonderful time creating amazing pictures from her writing and we are looking forward to seeing the film some time soon!

Irwell Valley Housing Association
The CEO of Irwell Valley Housing Association, Tom Manion, came to an assembly on 18 April to present us with a cheque for £1,500. The money will be used to reinforce and reward excellent behaviour and our School Council will meet to decide exactly what the money will be spent on.


ANIMALS INTUITION
In March 2013, Classes 4, 2 and 1 along with our Reception and Nursery children, enjoyed their visit from Andy, the exotic animal expert. The children were able to handle snakes, spiders and lizards, to name just a few!

SCIENCE BOFFINS
On Wednesday 27 March, The Science Boffins fascinated everyone with various science experiments during a Special Science Assembly. The children were shown many fun experiments and then joined the Boffins for an E-Energy workshop.



ECO WORKSHOPS
On 23 January 2013, every class took part in an Eco workshop, Fun with MACC. The children learned about climate change and it's effects and found out more about 'Re-ducing, Re-using and Re-cycling'.
We have purchased new recycling bins and energy monitors to enable the children to track and record electricity consumption in school. Our Eco Warriors, two members from each class, meet on the first Friday of each new half term in the library.

FIRE SERVICE- January 2013
 Three members of our local Fire Service came in to talk to Year 6 about how they respond to all emergency calls from the public.We discussed, as a class, the importance of having a fire alarm and an escape plan in our homes. They also explained that many emergency calls are not due to fires but to various incidents such as people trapped in vehicles following road traffic accidents.They are also called to deal with other domestic situations such as flooding, storm damage, people unable to gain access to premises and animals in distress.
They then gave the children a demonstration of how they put on their uniform, explaining how important the different items are in potentially dangerous situations.


VIKING VISIT
On 3 October 2012 our Keystage 2 children met a Viking! The children spent the morning learning all about Viking times, weapons, armour, houses, trade and coins. A fun time was had by all and best of all we learned lots of interesting new things about life as a Viking.



SCIENCE THEATRE GROUP
During September, Years 1 to 6 had a visit from the Kinetic Theatre Company who put on a science based production called Lady Cecily's Soundbox. The production was linked to the sound and waves area of the Science curriculum and included vibrations, frequency, amplitude, how sound travels & hearing and the speed of sound vs light. The production was great fun and allowed both the teachers and children to get involved.


ANIMALS INTUITION
During May 2012, Class 1 and Class 2 were lucky enough to have a visit from Ranger Andy who brought with him lots of rainforest and nocturnal animals. We learnt lots of interesting things and even got to hold a python called Mr Tickles!


LOAN SHARK ASSEMBLY
As part of Loan Sharks Month of Action, members of the Illegal Money Lending Team came in to talk to the children about the dangers of illegal money lending and promote the use of the Credit Union.
One of the Team  came dressed as a rather nasty looking Shark and the Police / PCSOs were in attendance as well. The children were then invited to take part in a colouring  competition with prizes awarded for the best Poster.

CHILDREN'S AUTHOR VISIT
As part of our 2012 World Book Day celebrations we were delighted to welcome Victoria Barry who came in to talk to the children about the process of writing a book - from creating an ideas board through to publication.
She read extracts from her book 'Darkmoor:The Darkness Discovered' and the children had an opportunity to ask her questions.

RECYCLING ROBOT
Recycler the Rapping Robot visited the school on 23 February 2012 when Shirley Barre from Wastewatch gave an assembly on how to Reduce, Reuse and Recycle.


CHILDHOOD MEMORIES of THE SECOND WORLD WAR
 Mr Jim Law, a grandparent and parishioner of St Margaret Ward, kindly agreed to come in to talk to the children and share some of his own experiences from the war years. Although he was only a small child at the time, Mr Law had many interesting stories to tell and the children listened intently before asking their own questions.

CYCLING PROFICIENCY
Each year,instructors from Trafford's Road Safety Unit come into school to run a three day course from the Safer Cycling Scheme.Year 6 children take this excellent opportunity to receive supervised ' on road' training, concentrating in particular on observation, starting and stopping and manoevres at junctions.
