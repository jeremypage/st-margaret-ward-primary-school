SCHOOL PLAYS AND CONCERTS

Summer Production 2013
This year's Leavers Production was "Goodbye my Friend".The children remembered their years at St Margaret Ward with a combination of witty one liners and beautiful singing. A few tears were shed (by parents) as the emotional theme tune ended this wonderful production. Well done, Mrs McGrath and Year 6.



Clarinet lessons - Class 4 enjoyed weekly clarinet lessons and then performed in front of the whole school and their proud parents on Friday 22 March.

Christmas productions 2012
Our KeyStage 2 children reminded us all of the true meaning of Christmas in their very moving production of
'May there always be Angels'.

Each class presented part of the Nativity Story in the style of a jigsaw which was completed with the birth of the Baby Jesus.

Children in Classes 1 and 2 joined forces in their wonderful production of 'Hey Ewe!'which followed the story of an inquisitive young sheep. One night, while all the other sheep are trying to sleep, some extraordinary events unfold before her!

Our Reception children delighted their audience with their wonderful performance 'The Stroppy Star'. Is the place at the top of the tree really the most important? All the decorations wanted to get a place on the Christmas tree, thinking that they were the most important. The children learnt through performing 'The Stroppy Star' that getting a place at the top is not the most important part of Christmas - it is of course, the birth of our saviour, Jesus Christ.




Flute Lessons - During the 2012 Spring term, the children in Class 4 learned how to play the flute. They worked really hard during their weekly music lessons and on Thursday 22 March they performed at a concert for their parents and the whole school.

Private Music Lessons - Being inspired by the whole class music tuition previously undertaken in school, we now have 13 children in Year 4 learning either saxaphone, clarinet or flute with a peripatetic music teacher. They are all very keen and we are hoping that this will be the start of more children learning to play a wider variety of musical instruments in the future.
