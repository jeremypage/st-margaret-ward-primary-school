SPORTS NEWS


School Sports Organising Crew

On Wednesday 6th November our new ‘School Sports Organising Crew’ (SSOC) attended a meeting at Ashton on Mersey High School. The SSOC are going to join together to organise and promote sport in our school. Their responsibilities will include organising resources, publicising events, updating the sport section of our school website and listening to the views of their peers. The meeting gave us lots of ideas to get started and we had the chance to work on our communication and leadership skills. We have decided that Clyde will be our school sport mascot and will join us for all of our sporting competitions and festivals.

Football

On Wednesday 6 November, a group of eleven children from Classes 3 and 4 took part in a football competition at Wellfield Junior School.
During the tournament we played against four other schools and came third overall.



2013 Summer Sports Festival
 St Margaret Ward school re-opened the school gates in the summer holidays for children of the school and the community to take part in a Summer Sports Camp.
The children participated in wide range of activities from erecting a tent to cheer leading. Football proved popular for the boys whilst X Factor contestants appeared and performed for the children which was a treat.
Overall it was a fantastic week with exceptional attitudes from all the amazing children who attended. Well done !


Manchester United Football Coaching
 Class 1 and Class 4 have enjoyed a Summer term of PE sessions with football coaches from Manchester United. They have learned lots of new skills and have taken part in mini tournaments.

Street Dance
 Year 3 and Year 4 were recently given the opportunity to attend an after-school Street Dance Club and up to 30 children attended each week. As well as being lots of fun, this club aimed to boost children's self esteem, coordination and fitness and complements our existing after-school clubs.
Lacrosse
 Sixteen children from Years 4, 5 and 6 took part in Lacrosse training over a seven week period. They learned different tactics and then used their newly developed skills to play a game during the last session.

Girls Football
 Our girls recently represented the school in a friendly match against Wellfield. All the girls who participated produced an exciting game of football showing lots of enthusiasm and good sportsmanship. A fantastic end to the term with all parents/carers and teachers showing fantastic support. Well done girls.

Tag Rugby Tournament
On Tuesday 15 January 2013, a group of our Year 5 and Year 6 children took part in a Tag Rugby Tournament at Woodheys School.They played a number of games against teams from four other primary schools and one of our teams got through to the final against St Michaels. The match ended with St Margaret Ward winning the competition and the children were thrilled to bring back their trophy. Congratulations to all the children.

Our St Margaret Ward boys football team recently took part in a number of tournaments for local primary schools and they have shown real commitment as well as a great team spirit during all their matches.

World Sports Day
On Monday 25 June, each class took part in our World Sports Day celebrations by choosing a country to support and we had lots of fun making flags and finding out about the culture of our chosen country. The day started with a show case assembly where each year group shared the work they had been doing. A child was then chosen from each class to take part in our'Olympic Torch Relay', having been nominated for demonstrating the Olympic Values. A Paralympics Games event took place the following week. Organised by sports coach Mr Quigley, this event gave the children the opportunity to take part in games such as chair volley ball, curling and bocca.

Football Teams
We are fortunate to have professional coaches in twice a week as part of our extra curriculum activities.On Tuesday our junior children train and on Wednesday it is the turn of the children from Year 1 and Year 2 to take part in the coaching sessions.Our boys and girls learn skills and technique and just as importantly, how to play the 'Great Game' in the correct sporting manner.

Sponsored Penalty shootout
Our annual fundraising event took place in June and due to the popularity of last years event, we decided to have another Sponsored Penalty Shootout.The children dressed in football kits and everyone got involved in the day as they tried to get the ball past the selection of goalkeepers.Over £1000 was raised thanks to the generosity of parents, families and friends.

Gymnastic Competition
During our 2012 Spring Term, Class 4 and Class 5 worked hard during their gym lessons, working in small groups to choreograph and perfect a full routine. They then took part in a competition in front of a panel of judges who chose the winners - the Human Bicycle from Class 5.

Paralympic sports
Prior to the 2012 Olympic and Paralympic games, our children participated in Paralympic events as part of their P.E lesson. These fresh new games helped the children develop their co-ordination, awareness and listening skills. Over the weeks, the children were involved in blind football, target goal, volleyball and outdoor adventures. These games were a fantastic way of raising awareness about the Paralympics and they encouraged the children to appreciate the value of their differences.

Tesco Sports Vouchers for Schools
We have been collecting Tesco vouchers to purchase PE equipment for our school and thanks to parents, parishioners and friends collecting, we have been able to purchase a wide range of outdoor games, toys and sports equipment for children of all ages to enjoy both during PE lessons and at lunchtimes.

Swimming
Throughout the year, our Junior children have swimming lessons in the club pool at Sale Leisure Centre. Each term,one class have a weekly swimming session in which they learn the basics of water safety and stroke improvement in small groups, taught by three experienced swimming coaches.Mr Ratchford, our Headteacher, himself a qualified swimming coach, accompanies the children.
