Supporting the Valley of the Moon Children's Centre
 At the beginning of the 2013 Autumn term, our Fair Trade Group organised a Children's Raffle to raise funds for one of our favourite charities, the Valley of the Moon Children's Centre in Mallasa, Bolivia. As one of our former pupils was going out there as a volunteer the following week, we had a very short time to run the raffle and although the tickets were on sale for only one week, there was an amazing response from all our school children, staff and friends of our school - Thank you to everyone who supported our fundraising!
On behalf of the children at St Margaret Ward School, Alice was able to bring the Children's Centre £150 along with a further donation of £25 raised by last year's Class 5 who collected pennies in a jar.

MAKE FOOD FAIR CAMPAIGN
As a Fairtrade School, we were asked by the Fairtrade Foundation to support the 2 million smallholder farmers who produce the majority of the world’s food but make up half of the world’s hungriest people. With ‘Food and hunger’ on the agenda of the G8 summit, this was an opportunity to petition the decision makers to give global support to those in need. We encoraged parents, staff and parishioners to either sign the paper petition or sign up online to create their own mini marchers. Groups of Mini Marchers were placed around school to draw attention to the campaign and we then forwarded the signed sheets on to the Fairtrade Foundation.

FAIRTRADE FORTNIGHT 2011
We organised a number of activities and events for Fairtrade Fortnight 2011 linked with the Fairtrade Foundation themes of 'Show off your Label' and Fairtrade Cotton.
With the successful renewal of our Fairtrade School status until 2012, our SMW Action Group were determined to make an even bigger and better impact!
Big Brew Coffee Morning and Bunting Decorating Event
On Tuesday 1 March 2011 our Reception and Class 1 children invited parents, grandparents and parishioners to join them for their Class Mass which was followed by a Big Brew Coffee morning when Fairtrade tea, coffee and orange juice along with a selection of Fairtrade biscuits and chocolate were on offer.There was also an opportunity to help the children decorate flags of cotton bunting which will become part of the Fairtrade Foundation's attempt to break the record for the longest line of hand decorated bunting.
The children had great fun and were delighted that so many adults took part in their Big Brew and activity morning - they certainly appreciated all the help in decorating their bunting.The morning was a real success and it was wonderful to see the generations come together -our children and parents said that they had really enjoyed working together and have a greater understanding of how easy it is for us to make a difference to the lives of people in the developing world simply by buying fairly traded goods.We are most grateful for the donations made on the morning which will be going to The Valley of the Moon Children's Centre in Bolivia.
We would like to say a huge thank you to The Co-operative Membership for their generous sponsorship of our Big Brew event.
Fairtrade Cotton Bunting Competition
This year we asked our children to decorate flags of cotton bunting as we are taking part in the Fairtrade Foundation's aim to break the world record for the longest line of hand decorated bunting. Some children worked on them at home but many worked on them together in class during Fairtrade Fortnight. Our Reception and Class 1 children even held a 'Bunting Decorating event' to involve parents and family members.
Although all the cotton flags have to be sent back to the Fairtrade Foundation, we have taken lots of photos of all the wonderful designs before facing the difficult task of selecting prize winners.
The winners were announced at our Thursday morning assembly and seemed delighted with their selection of Fairtrade chocolate.
We are grateful to The Co-operative Membership for providing vouchers for us to purchase all the Fairtrade prizes for the children.

Design a Fairtrade Teapot Competition
Our competition this year was influenced by the theme of The Big Brew and aimed at Key Stage 1 and Reception, who were invited to colour in their very own Fairtrade teapot. The children were very creative in their designs and it was so difficult to choose just one winner, we decided to have a winner and runner-up in each class! Every child who took part received a small chocolate and the main prizes were Fairtrade mini footballs or purses and chocolate bars.

World Fair Trade Day - 9 May 2009
The message this year -The impact you make is immense- was a salute to the people and organizations who have dedicated themselves to making Fair Trade what it is today.
To mark International Fair Trade Day, our Action Group organised a children's raffle. With the tickets priced at only 20p each, many children took the opportunity to join in and our team were kept busy selling tickets at morning break.
The £54 raised from the raffle is being donated to The Valley of the Moon Children's Centre in Mallasa,Bolivia

Our Fairtrade Action Group and our whole school celebrated the news that we were awarded Fairtrade School status in July 2008 and the children were thrilled to know that all their hard work and enthusiasm had been rewarded. For more information on the benefits of being a Fairtrade school, please take a look at Why become a Fairtrade School?
We became actively involved in Fair Trade in 2006 when representatives of our school council joined with other pupils from Trafford Schools for a Pupil Forum on Fair Trade, at Sale's Waterside Arts Centre.The day was organised by the Manchester Development Education Project and included drama, storytelling and creative art workshops,simulation games and information on becoming a Fair Trade school.
