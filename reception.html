We value the unique role of parents as a child's first educator and strive to involve parents in all aspects of their child's education.
In the Spring Term, as part of our topic, 'People who help us' parents were invited to come in and talk to the children about their job. They showed the children some of the tools and equipment they needed for their job and talked to the children about any training and qualifications they needed to follow their line of work.The children were highly motivated and excited to find out about the different aspects of the jobs discussed.

Athene Gill Banner's dad is a musician and during the summer term he brought in his saxaphone to play nursery rhymes and some classical pieces for the children. He explained the different parts of the instrument and the children asked lots of very sensible questions.

There was great excitement at the emergence of our butterflies from their cocoons. In Reception we took photographs each week watching the transformation from tiny caterpillars to beautiful painted lady butterflies.



The children in the Reception Class enjoyed taking part in a drama workshop which was videoed and watched later in class on the Interactive Whiteboard.
The whole class performed a short play entitled, The Toy Box.



The children love the role play area which helps to develop their personal, social and emotional skills along with their communication, language and literacy skills.
They particularly enjoyed our beauty salon where they could have facials, manicures and even hair styled by our top stylists. Boys and girls took on all roles in the salon.
The children loved playing in their Space Station which they also helped to create.

The children in Reception benefit from trips out of school to enhance their learning.



At St Margaret Ward we understand that being outside is both an educational and social experience. Children learn through play and movement and have more space to be physically more active outside.
We are committed to developing our outdoor play provision and have recently had a bike track laid. All areas of the Early Years Foundation Stage can be taught and nurtured outdoors.
