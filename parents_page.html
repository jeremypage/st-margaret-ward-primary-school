Story Sacks
We have received 5 Story Sacks which can now be borrowed by parents of children in Nursery and Reception. The kits include props such as puppets to bring stories to life and parents wishing to borrow the story sacks were invited to a short session in school to learn more of how to get the most from them. On Thursday 7 November, the session explained why sharing books together is so important and how the activities carried out at home link to the school curriculum. This was followed by a look at the resources and a story from one of the bags. Five of the families were able to borrow a story sack on the day and the remaining families have been placed on a waiting list.The afternoon was very successful with 13 families attending and we plan to hold another session at a different time to allow other parents the opportunity to attend.

Family Fun Days
Three family fun days took place in and around Sale West Community Centre during the 2012 summer holidays.The aim was to provide high quality, free holiday activities for the whole family in one place, offering happy, healthy opportunities for children and families to spend time together.Over 700 adults and children enjoyed a range of activities offering something for all age groups including Street Art, Family Olympics, Drumming, Games, Zoolab, Circus Skills, Toddler Play, Face Painting and Glitter Art. Nearly 70 families from St Margaret Ward School attended these very successful events and everyone had a wonderful time -

Budding Artists
As part of developing the planting and growing activities on offer for our children at St Margaret Ward, we were delighted to be able to offer some fun, interactive family sessions focusing on planting & growing for healthy eating. Budding Artists was a joint school/community art project which provided an opportunity for children in Year 4 and their family members to focus on healthy eating and the types of fruit and vegetables we can grow in this country.
The project took place simultaneously at 3 schools (St Margaret Ward, The Firs and Woodheys Primary schools) during January and February 2012. In the first 2 sessions delivered by Abigail Pound from Groundwork, we considered the elements needed to make planting successful; planted some fruit bushes outside and grew some seeds ready for planting outside in March.
The final 3 sessions were delivered by Fi Smith, a professional artist who worked with the families to produce some beautiful art boards, on the same theme, for display in the school grounds.
