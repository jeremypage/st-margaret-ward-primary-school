School Trips and Activities

ROBINWOOD- SEPTEMBER 2013
On 11 September 2013, a group of our junior children went on a three day residential trip to the Robinwood Activity Centre in Todmorden.The children had a fantastic time and were a credit to their parents and to the school. They took part in numerous, often challenging activities such as the 'Leap of Faith' and the 'Giant Swing' and worked well together in team building excercises.


WATER SAFETY COMPETITION

During the Summer 2013 term, our junior children took part in a Water Safety competition organised by Trafford Fire service and the entries were displayed in Fire stations across the borough during the holiday period. 
Two children from St Margaret Ward won prizes! Jack and Matthew (now in Y5) won 2 tickets each to the Sea life centre at the Trafford Centre. Well done to all, we were told the standard of entries was very high.


ORDSALL HALL- JULY 2013
Our Class 4 children enjoyed aTudor Living experience at Ordsall Hall.The children helped to prepare a Tudor banquet, explored the hall and ended the day by learning a traditional Tudor Rat Dance.



SEALIFE CENTRE- JULY 2013 As part of our Manchester topic, Class 5 visited one of our newest tourist attractions, Sealife Manchester. The children engaged in an action packed tour, witnessing up close some of the world's most endangered sea creatures and observed the magical happenings beneath the world's oceans. They hunted for special facts which also touched on our environmental studies and what we can do to save some of these wonderful creatures.We all thoroughly enjoyed ourselves.
As an extra special suprise for all the children, we arranged a visit to the neighbouring tasty treat shop, Krispy Kremes. The children loved watching the doughnuts being made but most of all, thoroughly enjoyed dipping and decorating their own tasty treat.

MOSSBANK PARK - JULY 2013
0ur Reception children had a wonderful day at Mossbank Park where they experienced the delights of the butterfly house and children's farm before playing in the adventure playground.


STOCKPORT AIR RAID SHELTER- JUNE 2013
This half term our Year 3 children enjoyed a visit to Stockport Air Raid Shelter. They spent the day exploring the tunnels and experiencing what life was like for an evacuee during the Second World War. They met two helpful Air Raid Wardens who told them what it was like in the tunnels during a raid. The children were particularly brave when experiencing the tunnels when the lights went out!

EASTER EGG DECORATING COMPETITION - MARCH 2013
Our School Council organised a competition for Easter and asked the children to get creative with their ideas for decorating eggs. The response was fantastic and all the wonderful creations were put on display in the entrance hall for everyone to admire. Prizes were awarded for the top three winners.

ECO WARRIORS PLANT AN ORCHARD IN SALE WEST - MARCH 2013
Our Eco Warriors (and a couple of their mums) went along to Sale West Community Centre on 21st March to help plant an orchard on what was once the site of a pub. We also went into the Comunity garden to learn how  potatoes are planted. We had a wonderful time and were very lucky with the weather. Irwell Valley Housing organised this and we were provided with a healthy, hearty snack afterwards.

Nursery - On Wednesday 12 December, parents and family members were invited into our Nursery for craft sessions with their children. The adults and children thoroughly enjoyed all the sticking, glueing and paperchain making and it is hoped that more craft sessions will be arranged for next year.

ST MARGARET WARD CHRISTMAS FAIR - 1 DECEMBER 2012
Our School Christmas Fair took place on Saturday 1 December and raised an amazing £2,400! Thank you to everyone who worked so hard to make the day such a great success, everyone who contributed prizes or stall items and all who came along on the day.

CLASS 2 STEP BACK IN HISTORY AT STAIRCASE HOUSE - OCTOBER 2012
As part of our topic, 'London Now and Then', Year 2 stepped back in history when we went on our school trip to Staircase House in Stockport.
The children had a fantastic time dressing up and experiencing what life would be like in 1666 (the time of 'The Great Fire of London') through role-play and hands-on activities. In small groups we used leather buckets and water squirters pretending to put the great fire out. We also buried wine and cheese to keep it safe just like Samuel Pepys did back in 1666. During the afternoon we all enjoyed making Tudor houses using wattle and daub. We all had a wonderful time and thoroughly enjoyed our step back in time.



FUNDRAISING FOR THE NORTH WEST AIR AMBULANCE - NOVEMBER 2012
A 'Name the Teddy' competition was held in school to raise funds for the North West Air Ambulance Charity and combined with a mini jewellery sale, a grand total of £126.50 was raised for this vital life saving service.
The Sainsbury's Sale store also invited us to take part in a colouring competition to raise the profile of the charity and kindly donated all the prizes. Children in Class 3, 4 and Brightstars decorated their helicopter pictures and the three winning entries were chosen by staff at Sainsbury's. Charlotte, Alfie and Paris were thrilled to be presented with their model helicopters, pens and Olympic caps at an assembly in front of the whole school.

MACMILLAN COFFEE MORNING - OCTOBER 2012
On Thursday 4 October, we held a 'World's Biggest Coffee Morning' event for Macmillan Cancer Support. Everyone was invited to come together in the school hall to enjoy coffee and cake and raise funds to pay for Macmillan nurses as they support people affected by cancer. As well as an own clothes day, all the children took part in a colouring competition. The winner was Clara in Class 1 whose colourful gingerbread man poster was chosen by the judges.
The morning was a great success and thanks to the hardwork of all those involved and the wonderful support of families and friends, the fantastic total of £552 was raised.




Previous Trips and Events
 
TATTON PARK FARM - JUNE 2012
On Tuesday 19 June, our Nursery children visited Home Farm at Tatton Park and they were lucky to see a one day old calf and some lovely baby chicks. The children thoroughly enjoyed their day and even managed to have their lunch outside in the picnic area as for once it didn't rain!

JUBILEE PICNIC - MAY 2012

As the Diamond Jubilee was during our Whit holidays, our children enjoyed an early celebratory Jubilee Picnic on Friday 25 May 2012, our last day of term. Everyone was encouraged to dress in red, white and blue and each class brought along their handmade bunting, decorations and crowns which all added to the celebrations. Our catering staff prepared a variety of jubilee themed picnic food including union jack cakes, fruit and juice, with decorated plates and cups. Games and activities were set up on the playing fields and despite the odd gust of wind blowing off the regal crowns and tiaras, the children had a wonderful time and the picnic was a great success.



MANCHESTER AIRPORT
During their exciting day at Manchester airport, Class 1 learned all about the different jobs that people do there and took part in role play activities. They also saw Concorde and then went on board a DC10 where they all had the chance to look around the cockpit and sit in the pilot's seat. The children enjoyed watching aircraft taking off and landing as they had their picnic then had time to play on the viewing area adventure playground.


THE BIG TIDY UP

Once again our school took part in the Sale West Big tidy Up event. This is an event which promotes a sense of belonging and pride in our local area. 8 children accompanied Mrs Murphy to join children from The Firs and Woodheys schools to complete a litter pick of the area on Thursday 8th March 2012.

ECO SCHOOL ACTIVITIES
In an effort to make our school and local area a more environmentally friendly place, we now have an Eco-Schools Committee at St Margaret Ward. The team of ECO-WARRIORS meet together every week to represent the “green” views of their school. The Eco-Warriors already have plans in place to make our paper recycling more efficient, cut down on the energy we waste and make sure that the grounds are free of litter.
We can be very proud of our Eco-Warriors at St Margaret Ward, who have shown great dedication and understand that saving our planet is everyone’s responsibility.
