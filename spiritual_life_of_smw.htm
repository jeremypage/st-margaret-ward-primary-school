The Way, the Truth and the Life
Our RE programme of study, 'The Way, the Truth and the Life' has been embedded into our curriculum and the children cover a variety of topics as they move through the school.
Topics include: 'Getting to know Jesus' in the Foundation Stage; 'Families and Celebrations' in Year 1 and 'The Good News' in Year 2.

SVP Sudan Baby Feeding Project 2012
The children at St Margaret Ward are encouraged to think about the people around the world who are less fortunate than themselves and support the SVP in their Sudan Baby Feeding appeal each Advent. Just £49 feeds a baby for one year so each class from Nursery to Year 6 was challenged to raise £49.
The children, with the support of their families, raised enough money to feed 10 babies in the Sudan for a year and each class was presented with a certificate by Patricia McCann, the St Margaret Ward SVP President.

Staff Mass The new school year got off to a good start with the staff from St Margaret Ward, Our Lady of Lourdes and All Saints all celebrating Mass together at Our Lady of Lourdes followed by refreshments in the hall.
The following week Father Ned then celebrated Mass here for all the children and we took this opportunity to celebrate the life of our patron saint, St Margaret Ward.
The Feast of St Margaret Ward is celebrated on 30th August but as this is during our summer break, we feel it is very important to mark the occasion in a special way and this year our celebration took place on 18 September 2012. After Mass, the children shared not just one but three huge sponge cakes.


Lent During Lent it is traditional for our school to ask the children to sacrifice something or do something extra and donate the money to CAFOD or to one of the charities connected to our school. During Lent, we held a 'Snacks for Smiles'collection along with an 'Own Clothes Day' when the children were encouraged to contribute to help those less fortunate than themselves. During Holy Week each of the classes held a variety of fundraising events including raffles and cake sales and a grand total of £457 was raised which was divided between the new medical centre in Peru and Father Mark's work in Malawi.

CAFOD - On 8 March 2012 Rosaleen Fagan from CAFOD shared with us the story of some children living in Africa who had lived without clean water for most of their lives. After receiving donations from CAFOD the village was able to fund a water source which has totally transformed their lives.
Inspired by these incredible stories, an own clothes day was held to raise money to help CAFOD with their wonderful work.

Cafod Harvest Fast Day
Our Cafod Harvest Fast day was celebrated in school through an own clothes day with the children bringing in tins of food to be distributed to Cornerstone in Manchester.The children learned about the Cornerstone Day Centre which helps people who are homeless, have no fixed address or are cut off from their families.
The Cafod website has been used by all classes to raise the childrens awareness of poverty around the world and how poverty, violence and injustice stop millions of children from doing things we take for granted.


Childrens Liturgy Group at St Margaret Ward
Each week at our 6.30pm Saturday Vigil Mass, a group of volunteer parents organise and run a 'Little Church' for children of all ages to take part in a Childrens Liturgy. They read the Gospel together and then join in a fun activity relevant to the Gospel message. All children are most welcome and we'd love to have more children and their families come to Saturday Evening Mass so please come along!

St Margaret Ward - Our Patron Saint

Margaret Ward was born in Congleton,Cheshire.
While working in London she heard that a priest was imprisoned and by making friends with the gaoler's wife, she visited Father Watson and brought him food.Eventually she smuggled in a rope and persuaded an Irish boatman, John Roche, to be ready to take the priest accross the River Thames. Although the priest escaped, the rope was accidentally left behind and traced to Margaret.
She was imprisoned,questioned and tortured and offered her freedom if she would surrender Father Watson and convert to the Church of England.She declined saying she was ready to lay down her life for conscience and loyalty to God and her Religion.
She was condemned to death for felony and sent to Tyburn on 30th August 1588 for execution by hanging.
She is one of the Forty Martyrs of England and Wales.
She was beatified in 1929 and canonised in 1970.
